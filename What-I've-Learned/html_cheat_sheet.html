HTML Quotation Tags/Elements

"blockquote": defines a section that is quoted from another source.

- the usual attribute with blockquote is a "cite"

- browser usually indent blockquote elements

- block-level tag/element

"q" or quotation tag/element: defines a short quotation

- inline tag/element

"abbr": stands for abbreviation or acronym

- marking abbreviations can give useful information to browsers, translation systems and search-engines

- the usual attribute is "title"

- inline element/tag

"address": defines the contact information for the author/owner of a document or and article

- the text in the "address" element usually renders in italic

- the browsers will always add a line break before and after the "address" element

- block-level tag/element

"cite": defines the title of a creative work

- usually renders in italic

- inline tag/element

"bdo": stand for Bi-Directional Override

- used to override the current text direction

- usual attribute is "dir" for direction

- inline tag/element
